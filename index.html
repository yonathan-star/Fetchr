Product premise

A dog-walking assistant that follows behind, reacts in real time, and handles the part everyone hates.

Docked loop

Return, recharge, analyze, and report without adding another chore.

What makes it different

Fetchr is not another pet gadget.

The idea is simple: remove the stop, the bag, the bend, and the carry. Fetchr follows the owner during the walk, watches for the exact event, performs collection, seals the waste, and resumes motion.

Movement that stays in the background

The robot is meant to disappear into the routine, keeping pace instead of demanding attention.

Cleanup plus health signal collection

Collected waste becomes useful data once the robot reaches the dock and sensors begin analysis.

Walk-following behavior

BLE RSSI tracking keeps the rover near the owner so the experience feels automatic rather than remote controlled.

Dual AI detection

One model watches posture, a second model checks the ground, reducing false triggers before the scoop cycle starts.

Mechanical collection

A custom servo-driven arm deploys, scoops into the onboard bin, and retracts so the owner never needs to pause.

Dock-based health insight

Color, moisture, and weight sensing extend the system beyond cleanup into lightweight wellness monitoring.

System flow

A cleaner walk in four steps.

01. Follow

The rover trails the owner and dog during the walk, staying close enough to respond immediately.

02. Detect

Model one flags posture, model two confirms waste, and the system only acts after both signals align.

03. Scoop

The robot stops, deploys the arm, completes collection, seals the waste, and retracts the mechanism.

04. Dock

Once the walk is over, the dock handles charging and starts basic analysis for companion-app reporting.

Design target

The experience should feel less like operating a robot and more like adding a silent assistant to an existing habit.

PETG

Custom 3D-printed structural parts designed for the prototype.

ESP32

Onboard control and connectivity for detection, movement, and dock communication.

Why this matters

The routine is universal. The solution still does not exist.

Dog owners already accept an annoying ritual because there has not been a credible product that handles the cleanup during the walk itself. Fetchr is aimed at that exact gap.
